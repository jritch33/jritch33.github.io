Family health history (FHx) and hereditary cancer risk

We collected family health histories (FHx) for 4,914 probands (a person serving as the starting point for the genetic study of a family).

Credit: All Probands by Jordon Ritchie

2,223 of the FHx's we collected met hereditary cancer criteria from either The American Medical College of Genetics and Genoimmcs (ACMG) or The National Comprehensive Cancer Network (NCCN) or both.

Credit: Probands At Risk by Jordon Ritchie

2,691 of the FHx's we collected DID NOT met hereditary cancer criteria from either The American Medical College of Genetics and Genoimmcs (ACMG) or The National Comprehensive Cancer Network (NCCN).

Credit: Probands Not At Risk by Jordon Ritchie

621 probands reported personal or family genetic testing and 156 of the FHx's we collected reported a positive personal or family genetic mutation.

Credit: Probands with a reported gene mutation by Jordon Ritchie

Of the 2,691 probands who did not meet criteria, the majority (90.6%) had at least one cancer in their FHx and just under three quarters (71.7%) had 1-3 cancers in their FHx.
